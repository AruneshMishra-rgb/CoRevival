üî• What is Coral Bleaching?

When corals are stressed by rising temperatures, they expel the colorful algae that live in their tissues and provide them with food. This turns them white - or "bleached."

ü™∏

Healthy Coral

Vibrant, colorful, teeming with life. Symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) provide nutrients through photosynthesis.

ü§ç

Bleached Coral

White, stressed, vulnerable. Algae expelled due to heat stress. Coral begins to starve without its food source.

üí° The Science Behind the Solutions

üî¨ Heat-Shock Proteins (HSPs)

These molecular "chaperones" help corals survive temperature stress by protecting cellular proteins from damage. Scientists are engineering corals to produce more HSPs.

‚öóÔ∏è Reactive Oxygen Species (ROS) Management

Heat stress creates harmful molecules that damage coral cells. New treatments use enzymes like catalase to neutralize these toxins.

üß¨ CRISPR & Gene Editing

Cutting-edge gene editing allows scientists to enhance coral resilience while maintaining strict biosafety protocols with kill-switches.
